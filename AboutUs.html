About us
Zeyton Nutraceuticals, Lda. is a technological start-up specialised in 100% natural hydroxytytrosol, the olive polyphenol with clinically-proven cholesterol protecting properties. Its patented green technology to extract hydroxytyrosol from olive oil industry products was developed in collaboration with IBET (www.ibet.pt ) and Nova, the New University of Lisbon.
Zeyton Nutraceuticals was established in 2013, bringing together scientists from Nova and seasoned business angels. Its production facilities are located side by side with a high quality olive oil producer, its main raw-material provider.
Our Mission
Zeyton Nutraceuticals develops innovative and environmentally friendly technologies to produce 100% natural hydroxytyrosol-rich extracts from high quality south Portuguese olives. It focuses on tailoring these extracts to optimise hydroxytyrosol clinically-proven cholesterol protective properties and boost the capacity of those extracts to prevent cardiovascular disease.
Our Goal
Zeyton Nutraceuticals aims at providing high quality, wholly natural extracts from obtained from olives paste, immediately after oil extraction.
These extracts are:
1) rich in olive polyphenols and standardised by their content in hydroxytyrosol, the anti-oxidant that protects cholesterol from oxidation and helps prevent cardiovascular diseases;
2) production takes place at the mill itself, provided with the latest equipments and conditions of excellence, side by side with olive oil. It is obtained using only water and clean filtration processes that are highly selective for hydroxytyrosol, avoiding contamination by any harmful substances. Zeyton Nutraceuticals continues to develop its patented processes through an extensive network of R&D collaborations, and in particular with IBET, a Biotechnology research platform, REQUIMTE, a Green Chemistry research centre of excellence, and Nova, the New University of Lisbon.

Processes and Patents
Process
Zeyton Nutraceuticals produces natural extract obtained from olives paste, immediately after oil extraction, using clean technology that respects the environment. Zeyton Nutraceuticals operates at a Portuguese olive mill, located in the south of the country, side by side with olive oil production.
There are no solvents or other chemical products used in the modular production process. The operations basically consists in physical separations, involving contact with inert materials. Used for several years in the food industry, these operations (centrifuging, filtering with membranes, drying) follow an innovative and simple enchainment allowing to obtain the main anti-oxidant ingredients from olive oil with health benefits. These properties were proved in the technical application delivered to the USA Patent Office that granted the American patent in 2011.
The extraction process has also been granted patents in Europe, Canada, South Africa, Algeria, Tunisia Morocco, and Brazil.
Zeyton’s patents describes a very efficient process to extract olive’s most beneficial components from the semi-solid cake that results from virgin olive oil production.
It is a totally clean process. It adds only water to the cake, and no solvents or any other type of additives, and uses filtration with special membranes. These (nano)filtration membranes selectively let hydroxytyrosol pass through and retain most other cake components, including all deleterious substances that might have been absorbed from the environment or added by agricultural practices. The obtained extract is therefore totally safe.
Zeyton’s process simplicity is one of its main advantages, as a one-step filtration yields a highly enriched aqueous solution of hydroxytyrosol, which is further concentrated by reverse osmosis, a standard process commonly used to produce fresh water. A comparison of results with other more complex processes proposed in the technical literature has proved that Zeyton´s process is twice more effective in extracting hydroxytyrosol from a given amount of olive cake. Moreover, Zeyton’s extracts have higher antimicrobial activity and in vitro cellular antioxidant activity, even when the hydroxytyrosol amounts are adjusted to equivalent values.
